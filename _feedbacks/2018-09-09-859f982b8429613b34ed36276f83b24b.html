I read your post https://graphqlme.com/2018/05/13/transactions-mutations-and-graphql/

And I found it a bit too general and a little startling. I am really bothered about the fact that GraphQl has no transactions and I find it hard to believe I have to rethink all DB modelling tasks.

Take for example a DB type that involves several arrays with complex children. No matter what's your schema you always end up with some type like these.

Now, it is easy to expect a use case that requires you to make copies of such an object. Now, go ahead and do it without transactions or, I'll be happy to see a schema design option that eliminates such a problem.

Regards,
Arie Fishler
